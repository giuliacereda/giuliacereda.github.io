Welcome

I am an Associate Professor of Statistics at the University of Florence (SSD SECS-S/01 – Statistics). My research lies at the intersection of statistical modelling and forensic science, with a focus on the methodological aspects of the evaluation of DNA matches through likelihood ratios and the rare type match problem.

My main research interests include the development of probabilistic frameworks for forensic inference, especially in challenging scenarios such as rare type matches and low-template DNA. I have worked extensively on the definition and evaluation of likelihood ratios from both frequentist and Bayesian perspectives, including the use of object-oriented Bayesian networks to model uncertainty in complex DNA profiles.

More recently, I have been focusing on profiling through SNP data and methylation data. My approach blends theoretical rigour with applied insight, contributing tools that are interpretable, ethically grounded, and applicable in real-world forensic and biomedical contexts.

I have also contributed to multiple studies using compartmental models and Bayesian meta-analysis to inform public health decisions, particularly during the COVID‑19 pandemic

I teach Statistics and Statistics applied to Forensic Science at both Bachelor’s and Master’s level.
