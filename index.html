I am an Associate Professor of Statistics at the University of Florence (SSD SECS-S/01 â€“ Statistics). My work lies at the intersection of statistical modeling, genetics, and forensic science, with applications ranging from DNA mixture interpretation to epigenetic profiling and population-level health modeling.

My main research interests include the development of probabilistic frameworks for forensic inference, especially in challenging scenarios such as rare type matches and low-template DNA. I have worked extensively on the definition and evaluation of likelihood ratios from both frequentist and Bayesian perspectives, including the use of object-oriented Bayesian networks to model uncertainty in complex DNA profiles.

More recently, I have been focusing on epigenomic data analysis, particularly using DNA methylation to infer behavioural or environmental exposures. My approach blends theoretical rigour with applied insight, contributing tools that are interpretable, ethically grounded, and applicable in real-world forensic and biomedical contexts.
