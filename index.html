I am an Associate Professor of Statistics at the University of Florence (SSD SECS-S/01 â€“ Statistics). My work mainly lies at the intersection of statistical modeling and forensic science, with particular interest in the methodological aspects of the evaluation of DNA match through likelihood ratios and evaluation of rare type match problem .

My main research interests include the development of probabilistic frameworks for forensic inference, especially in challenging scenarios such as rare type matches and low-template DNA. I have worked extensively on the definition and evaluation of likelihood ratios from both frequentist and Bayesian perspectives, including the use of object-oriented Bayesian networks to model uncertainty in complex DNA profiles.

More recently, I have been focusing on profiling through SNPs data , and methylayion data . My approach blends theoretical rigour with applied insight, contributing tools that are interpretable, ethically grounded, and applicable in real-world forensic and biomedical contexts.
